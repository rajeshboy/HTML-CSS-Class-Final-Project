Raj's Organic

I was born in a farming community in Nadi which is a city in Fiji Islands. My father was a wealthy farmer. We planted corn, sugarcane, rice, coconut, bananas, papaya, oranges, mangoes, tomatoes, okra, eggplant, pepper, potatoes, sweet-potatoes, pumpkin, cassava, yams, beans, peas, cabbage, bokchoy, lettuce, cauliflower, and some other greens, and herbs. Currently, I am running a vegetable farm at Hillsborough in North Carolina. It's called Raj's Organic. We plant 3 seasonal crops, that are Spring, Summer, and Fall. Our farm is certified organic and we build our own organic compost on the farm.

Spring Farming

We start Spring farming in early February and most of our Spring crops are ready for sale by mid of March. We plant all varieties of greens, some beans and peas, some herbs, and radishes and turnips for spring. Our spring and summer CSA starts on the first week of March. If interested, please sign-up by February 28th.

Summer Farming

Our summer farming starts soon after the last frost in April. We plant tomatoes, okra, eggplant, pepper, potatoes, sweet-potatoes, squash and pumpkin, beans, peas, and some herbs for summer. A farm tour day will be held at our farm on the second Saturday in May.

Fall Farming

In the fall we plant varieties of greens, lettuces, radishes, turnips, scallions, fall squash and pumpkin, and some herbs. We will offer a fall and winter CSA which will start on the first week of October. Details will be provided as time comes near.

Farming Links

Sugarcane Farming in Fiji

Sugarcane is a tropical perennial crop, and is planted in trenches for about 4 to 6 inches deep. The land is prepared with tractor driven plough in flat areas and bullock or horse carted plough is used in hilly areas. The harvesting season is mostly in the drier months when the sugar content in the cane is higher. Sugarcane is harvested by machines as well as manually with a cane-knife. Sugarcane is transported to the sugar mills by trucks as well as locomotive pulled carts. The juice from the sugarcane is extracted and processed to make sugar.

Farming in Hawaii

Hawaii has a tropical climate which is suitable for a lot of vegatables and fruits. Bananas, oranges, papayas, mangoes, coconuts, and other tropical fruits grow well in Hawaii. The climate there is very good for tropical perennials as they grow well after the harvest. Hawaii is also doing permanent agriculture with coconut, oranges, papayas, guavas, mangoes, and other fruits. Vegetables like Eggplant, Tomatoes, Pepper, Okra and others, last longer in that climate.

Farming Method

  1. Mow the farm with the tractor driven mower.
  2. Break the hard soil on the field with the tractor driven fieldbreaker.
  3. Plough the field with the tractor driven plough to break the soil clots.
  4. Till the soil with the tractor driven tiller to soften the soil.
  5. Use the tractor driver bedmaker or trenchmaker to make the beds or trenches for planting.
  6. Plant as per the seed instructions.
